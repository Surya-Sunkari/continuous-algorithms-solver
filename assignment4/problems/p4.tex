\section*{Problem 4}

Let $f : \mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{R}$ be $L$-smooth and $\mu$-strongly convex, and suppose we know $\mathbf{x}^\star := \operatorname{argmin}_{\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^d} f(x)$.
Let $\pi^\star : \mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{R}_{>0}$ be the density satisfying $\pi^\star(\mathbf{x}) \propto \exp(-f(\mathbf{x}))$. Give a density $\pi_0$ depending only on $\mathbf{x}^\star$, $\mu$, and $L$, that is $\beta$-warm with respect to $\pi^\star$, for
\[
\beta = \exp(O(d \log \kappa)), \text{ where } \kappa := \frac{L}{\mu}.
\]
