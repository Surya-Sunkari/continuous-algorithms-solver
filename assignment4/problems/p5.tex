\section*{Problem 5}

Let $\beta \geq 1$ and $\epsilon \in (0,1)$, and let $\{\mathcal{T}_x\}_{x \in \mathbb{R}^d}$ be transition distributions corresponding to a random walk on $\mathbb{R}^d$ with stationary distribution $\pi^\star$. Assume that for any density $\pi$ on $\mathbb{R}^d$ which is $\frac{\beta}{\epsilon}$-warm with respect to $\pi^\star$, $D_{\mathrm{TV}}(T\pi, \pi^\star) \leq \frac{1}{4}$. Prove that if $\pi_0$ is $\beta$-warm with respect to $\pi^\star$,\footnote{This gives a continuous generalization of Corollary 1, Part XIV.}
\[
D_{\mathrm{TV}}\left(T^k \pi_0, \pi^\star\right) \leq \epsilon \text{ if } k \geq \log_2\left(\frac{1}{\epsilon}\right).
\]
